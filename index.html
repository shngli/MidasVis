MIDAS Research Network Visualization

The Michigan Institute for Data Science (MIDAS) is a new multidisciplinary department at the University of Michigan, created in July 2015 as part of the university's Data Science Initiative. MIDAS aims to maintain a thriving data science community on the UM campus by fostering collaborative cross-cutting methodological and applied research. It will also coordinate data science education at the university, promote data science infrastructure on UM campus, and engage with industry on data science applications.

MIDAS is comprised of an interdisciplinary faculty of data scientists, from computer science and engineering, statistics, information science, public health, mathematics, and a range of other academic departments. Active data science research at MIDAS varies from data management, machine learning, data visualization, and addressing problems in disease model discovery, healthcare policy, materials synthesis, biology and social sciences.

The MIDAS Research Visualizer focuses on the connections among its faculty: how deparments areas connect to academic fields, how academic fields to research methodologies, and how research methodologies connect with faculty. The goal is to funnel visitors to in-depth content about MIDAS by presenting faculty connections in a concise and visual manner.
